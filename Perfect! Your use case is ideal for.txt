**1. Reconstructed "Normal" Image**: After training on normal images and mapping your test image to latent space, the generator will produce `G(z_Γ)` - what it thinks the image *should* look like if it were normal. This is essentially the model's best attempt to reconstruct your image using only patterns it learned from healthy/normal examples.

**2. Residual/Deviation Map**: The pixel-wise difference between your input and the reconstructed image: `x_R = |x - G(z_Γ)|`. ## Key Advantages for Your Use Case

1. **No manual annotation needed**: You just need a collection of normal images. The model learns what "normal" means automatically.

2. **Quantitative comparison**: The residual map gives you pixel-level error magnitudes, not just binary "anomaly/normal" labels.

3. **Visual interpretability**: You can see both what the model thinks is normal AND where deviations occur, making it easy to understand the model's reasoning.

4. **Unknown anomalies**: The model can detect anomalies it has never seen before, as long as they deviate from the learned normal distribution.

## Dataset Considerations for Your Images

- **Ensure training set is truly "normal"**: Carefully curate your training images to exclude any with anomalies, otherwise the model will learn to generate those anomalies as normal
